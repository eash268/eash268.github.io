MUSC 030-060

Final Project: My Annotated Playlist

12/14/20 9:26 PM   |   18 min read   |   Jump To: Overview

Overview

Welcome to my personal annotated playlist! Here are a few tracks I find meaningful. They all come from SoundCloud because that is where I listen to most of my music. Feel free to press the play button next to any of the tracks below; please bear in mind that all of these works come from a community of talented Indian artists all over the country and expect to hear remixes and mashups of popular Western songs with popular Bollywood and Punjabi Bhangra music. Enjoy!

Patakay: A Bhangra Fusion Mixtape

This mixtape is hands-down one of my favorite tracks of the year. It was created by my friend Aranya Nandi, and his skillful combination of melodic Bhangra songs with intense Western songs to create a high-octane and euphoric feel while listening is simply brilliant. Nandi was born in 1999 went to school in Doha, Qatar. His DJ name is Bassdoctor and this is by no means his first production. Since dancing for OSU Genesis, he fell in love with the Bhangra portion of all dance sets and decided to make a tribue to them by making a full mixtape of Bhangra mixes. The piece was created using a production software called Ableton and Nandi perfected techniques like compression and limiting to create this beautifully engineered piece. I've personally selected it because some of my best memories this Fall have been associated with listening to it and because it makes me feel very energetic and happy.

Penn Dhamaka 2016 Mixtape

This mixtape was created by me and my friend Pranav Kunapali when I was a freshman in college for our dance team, Penn Dhamaka. Every large college in the country has Indian fusion dance teams, and the total community around the country is over 11,000 members. To advertize things like performances and competitions, people pay producers to make mixtapes and release them on SoundCloud, like this one which was meant to advertize our Fall 2016 show. Pranav, artist name PRNU, grew up in the suburbs of Pennsylvania and studied carnatic music growing up. He has been producing since college and taught me how to produce my freshman year. Together, we created this track and it remains one of my favorite projects that I have worked on to this day. I chose it because this was the first piece I thought of when we learned about Topos, and I was trying to see if I could recognize any in here.

Klasikhz - Teri Khair Mangdi

This is one of the first remixes that ever got me interested in mashups. The "Don't Tell Em" instrumental sounds absolutely beautiful with Teri Khair Mangdi, a famous Bollywood song. The artist, Klasikhz, is a friend of friends and a very talented full-time producer. He is Punjabi and his mixes tend to be very Punjabi/Bhangra heavy. This mix was made for two very famous and talented dancers named Poonam and Priyanka, and the dance video is fantastic as well. I chose this song because I repeatedly come back to it when I need to anchor myself on what high quality mixes sound like and why they work. Notice the keys matching, the chord progressions matching, the BPMs being perfectly aligned, and a beautiful surprise instrument at the end that I won't ruin.

BurjKhalifa (Southside Remix) | #BurjKhalifaRemixContest

There is no way I could make an annotated playlist (or any playlist for that matter) without including at least one track by Dr. Srimix. Srihari Sritharan (Dr. Srimix) is a pioneer and a legend in the Indian fusion music space. He has been mixing since his time dancing with Brown Badmaash, an all male Indian fusion team at Brown University. Sri has been my friend for 5 years and taught me how to mix and live DJ. His brain is truly uniqely gifted to be able to listen to a song and match it with a different song that would mix well and create a masterpiece. This track that I chose is by far not one of my favorite of his works because everything else is just so good, but this was a track that he created recently as a remix of a popular new Bollywood song called Burj Khalifa. He has always been heavily influenced by 90s hiphop and brought that flavor to this remix as well. I chose this track specifically because it shows his range as both a mashup artist and a producer, and it is also a great track.

Silk Road Punjabi Mixtape

Margib is only of the most eccentric and unique Bollywood fusion artists I've met. He hails from UPenn and was a singer on our acapella team, Penn Masala. He is very interested politics, foreign relations, and news. He now mixes songs from all different sources and countries. In this track, you get to hear remixes with Tajik, Turkish, Kazakh, and Uzbek songs. I chose it due to this variety in music!
