The Host




Dr. Bala is a Board Certified Cataract and Eye Surgeon and a Fellowship-Trained Glaucoma specialist.

Dr. Bala is a native of the Inland Empire and grew up in Redlands, CA. She attended Redlands High School and was a state-ranked tennis player in high school. She completed her Bachelor of Arts undergraduate studies at Washington University in St. Louis in Anthropology and Biology, graduating with honors.

She attended Virginia Commonwealth University School of Medicine in Richmond, Virginia, where she developed an interest in ophthalmology and received the Arnold Gold Award in Humanism. She went onto complete her residency training in ophthalmology at the premier medical institution in the country, the Mayo Clinic in Rochester, Minnesota. She also completed her internship at Mayo Clinic in Rochester, Minnesota. While at Mayo, she authored over 10 peer-reviewed articles in several leading ophthalmology journals, served as President of the Mayo Fellows Association where she helped shape policy for trainees across all three Mayo Clinic sites. In addition, she received the Attitude, Commitment and Excellence award as a resident at Mayo Clinic. She went on to complete a one year medical and surgical glaucoma fellowship at University of California, Davis in Sacramento, CA where she was recognized by the department for her clinical research in minimally invasive glaucoma surgery (MIGS).

She was on staff at the Mayo Clinic in Rochester, Minnesota for 1 year when she and her husband decided to return home to the Inland Empire in Southern California. She is a member of the American Academy of Ophthalmology and the American Glaucoma Society. She regularly participates in community events in the Inland Empire and is committed to serving the community she grew up in to provide excellent eye care. She is an expert in advanced and premium cataract surgery and minimally invasive glaucoma surgery.
